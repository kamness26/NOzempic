🔥 The Roast
Let's talk about Kam. A man who has, at various points in his life, described himself as "athletic." Sir. Your metabolic age is 45. You are 42. Your body has aged three years faster than your actual birthday has arrived. That's not a metabolism, that's a time machine headed in the wrong direction.

You lost 1.8 pounds this week, which is respectable, but let's be honest — you have 57.6 pounds to go to hit your optimal weight. At this pace you'll be ready for swimsuit season in approximately 2027. Nili lost less weight but burned twice as much relative fat. You are hauling more cargo and moving it less efficiently. That's not a body composition issue. That's a logistics problem.

Your visceral fat is 16. Optimal is under 9. Visceral fat is the kind that wraps around your organs. Your organs are currently insulated. They are warm. They are comfortable. They would like you to stop feeding them.

We believe in you, Kam. We also believe in honesty. See you Thursday.
🧠
Fact of the Week
Visceral fat isn't just a cosmetic issue — it's metabolically active tissue that secretes inflammatory chemicals linked to insulin resistance, heart disease, and elevated cortisol. A visceral fat score of 16 (like someone we know) falls in the "high risk" zone. The good news: visceral fat is also the first type to go when you combine cardio with a calorie deficit. It responds faster than subcutaneous fat. One hour of zone 2 cardio per week can measurably move that number within a month.

🎯 Your Focus This Week
🫀
Attack That Visceral Fat First
Your visceral fat score of 16 is the single most important number to move. It responds faster than subcutaneous fat and has outsized health impact. The formula: 3 sessions of zone 2 cardio this week (30–45 min each at a pace where you can hold a conversation). Walking briskly counts. Cycling counts. Don't overthink it — consistency beats intensity at this stage.
🧮
Use Your BMR as the Starting Point
Your BMR is 2,069 kcal/day — that's what your body burns at rest, just keeping the lights on. Add in your actual activity and you're burning significantly more. A modest 500 kcal/day deficit (roughly one meal) puts you at about −1 lb/week without starving yourself. Track what you eat for just 3 days this week to establish a real baseline. Not forever — just 3 days.
💪
Protect Your Muscle Mass
Here's the good news you earned: your muscle mass is legitimately above standard across all segments. Arms, trunk, legs — all Standard or above. That's real. Protect it by keeping protein intake high (aim for 0.7–1g per lb of goal bodyweight ≈ 116–166g/day) and not dropping calories so aggressively that your body starts burning muscle. Losing fat while keeping muscle is the goal. You've got the engine — don't blow it up.
